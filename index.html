Mattupetty

The journey from Munnar to Mattupetty takes approximately 30 minutes by road and covers a distance of around 13 kilometers. Along the way, you'll be treated to breathtaking landscapes, winding roads, and lush greenery. The route passes through tea gardens, spice plantations, and small villages, providing a glimpse into the region's rural charm.

Valleys and Forest

Chinnar

Chinnar Wildlife Sanctuary is nestled in the Western Ghats and is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna. The sanctuary is particularly known for its population of vulnerable and endangered species, including the Indian star tortoise, grizzled giant squirrel, and the endangered Nilgiri tahr. If you're lucky, you might spot some of these remarkable creatures during your visit.

Pothamed

Pothamedu is known for its panoramic views, making it a favorite spot for nature lovers, photographers, and those seeking tranquility amidst the lush greenery. From the viewpoint, you can feast your eyes on the expansive tea gardens spread across the undulating slopes, creating a stunning visual treat.
